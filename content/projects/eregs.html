eRegulations

eRegs has been highlighted by the White House (for "mak[ing] regulations easier to read"), by CFPB's director, Richard Cordray (as helping regulations be "understood by the general public"), by CFPB's deputy director, Steve Antonakes (who described it as "intuitive, easy-to-navigate"), and brought up to a United States House of Representatives subcommittee.
